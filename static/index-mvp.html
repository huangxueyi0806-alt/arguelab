2026-06-11 · AI 与学术诚信

① 今日议题背景

多所大学开始允许学生在作业中使用 AI，但评分标准尚未统一。这场争论的核心不是「能不能用」，而是「用了之后，什么才算你的思考」。

② 外刊核心段落

"The University of Cambridge has announced that students may use AI tools in assessed coursework, provided they declare their use. The move has divided faculty, with some arguing it represents a necessary adaptation to a changing world, others warning it risks eroding the very skills a university education is meant to cultivate."
— The Guardian, 2026-06-10

③ 5 个可迁移表达

表达功能例句
provided (that) 条件限定 AI tools are allowed, provided students declare their use.
divided faculty 描述分歧 The proposal has divided faculty across departments.
represents a necessary adaptation 正面论证 This is not a compromise but a necessary adaptation.
risks eroding 负面后果 Over-reliance risks eroding critical thinking skills.
meant to cultivate 目的表达 These are the skills a liberal education is meant to cultivate.

④ 中文观点 → 英文 Argument Chain

中文观点：「允许用 AI 是好事，但学校必须教学生怎么用，而不是只说可以用。」

Thesis: Permitting AI use is a constructive step, but universities must go further.
↓
Premise 1: Permission without guidance creates a false equivalence between using AI and thinking.
↓
Premise 2: What needs to be taught is not how to prompt, but how to evaluate AI output.
↓
Counter-argument: Some argue this is inevitable—we cannot put the genie back in the bottle.
↓
Response: True, but "inevitable" does not mean "unguided".
↓
Conclusion: The goal is not to ban AI, but to ensure it remains a tool, not a substitute for thought.

⑤ 输出任务

写作（TEM-8 / IELTS Task 2）： Some people think universities should allow students to use AI tools in their assignments. To what extent do you agree or disagree? （建议 280–320 词）

口语（IELTS Part 3）： Do you think AI will change how we define "original work"? Explain your view. （建议 1–1.5 分钟）
